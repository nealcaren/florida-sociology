Florida adopted a modified version of the OpenStax Introduction to Sociology textbook, then cut it nearly in half. Entire chapters on race, gender, sexuality, and economic inequality were removed. What remained was systematically edited: names deleted, data softened, critical frameworks replaced with vague gestures toward "more advanced courses."

The changes fall into four patterns.

Each of these changes is small enough to miss on its own. Taken together, they amount to a different textbook, one that avoids naming racism, erases LGBTQ people, softens inequality data, and removes the theoretical tools students would use to analyze power. The original is an introduction to sociology. The Florida version is what remains after the discipline's hardest questions have been cut.
